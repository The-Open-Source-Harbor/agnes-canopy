================================================================================
  1. USER PERSONAS AND USE CASES
================================================================================

1.1 Community Resident

Someone living in Baltimore who wants to understand their neighborhood relative
to the rest of the city. Needs a simple, engaging interface, not a government
database. Interface should feel personal to Baltimore, not generic.

Use case: A resident in East Baltimore sees their tract has the highest SNAP
participation rate in the city. They share this with a local nonprofit applying
for food access funding. The data provides concrete justification.

Design: No technical jargon. Neighborhood names alongside tract IDs. Mobile-
friendly. Highlight actionable context alongside data.

1.2 Funder / Policymaker

A foundation program officer, city council member, or government agency staff
who needs to assess where to direct resources. Needs city-wide disparity views,
evidence to cite in reports or grant applications.

Use case: A foundation considers a broadband grant. They select the broadband
indicator, see which tracts have the lowest access, and write a needs assessment.

Design: Export-ready maps and charts. Downloadable data. Methodology documented.

1.3 Community Health Worker

A field-based health worker conducting outreach. Needs neighborhood-level risk
indicators (poverty, food insecurity, uninsured rate, housing instability) and
the ability to flag high-need tracts for prioritized visits.

Use case: A worker sees a tract has the highest uninsured rate and lowest income
in the city. They schedule an additional outreach visit and prepare referrals.

Design: Neighborhood Explorer is the primary view. Social determinants prominent.
Future: field data collection interface linking to More Than a Shop partnership.

1.4 Researcher / Academic

A public health or urban economics researcher studying Baltimore. Needs the
full indicator dataset downloadable, pipeline documentation for reproducibility,
and correlation/spatial analysis tools.

Use case: A PhD student downloads the 91-indicator CSV, merges with survey data,
and cites the pipeline documentation in their methods section.

Design: Researcher login with CSV download and API access. Data dictionary
and pipeline docs linked from dashboard.

1.5 Clinician / Care Coordinator (Agnes integration, future)

A clinician or care manager in Agnes reviewing a specific patient. Needs to see
neighborhood-level social determinants for the patient's census tract to inform
care planning and referrals.

Use case: A care coordinator reviews a patient with uncontrolled diabetes. Agnes
passes the patient's tract to Canopy, which shows above-average food insecurity
and below-average income, shaping the care plan.

Design: Agnes SSO passes tract ID automatically. Clinician view is a focused
single-tract summary. See Section 4 for Agnes integration.

================================================================================
  2. VIEWS AND NAVIGATION
================================================================================

2.1 Current Views (implemented)

  City Overview (Map & Data)
    Scatter map of 199 tracts colored by selected indicator. Side-by-side data
    table (top 50 tracts). Histogram and box plot. Sidebar: category + indicator.

  Neighborhood Explorer
    Select a census tract. Key metrics with delta vs. city average. Housing and
    economic panel. Health and education panel. Radar chart vs. city average.

  Indicator Analysis
    X/Y scatter plot with OLS trendline. Correlation coefficient with label.

  About
    Data sources, data quality note, methodology.

2.2 Planned Views

  Regional Economic Context: BLS and FRED time-series as a tab within the
    main app. Labor market trends, CPI, home price index for Baltimore MSA.

  Community Voice (future): Resident-reported data from Priscilla/Homeulo.
    Toggle: compare government-reported vs. resident-reported by tract.

2.3 Navigation

  Current: radio buttons in the left sidebar.
  When views expand: top nav bar (Overview | Neighborhoods | Indicators |
    Economic Context | Community Voice | About). Sidebar for filters.

================================================================================
  3. ACCESS LEVELS AND ROLES
================================================================================

  Current: No login. Fully public. Correct for this phase.

  Phase 2 roles:

  Public (no login): City Overview, Neighborhood Explorer, Indicator Analysis,
    About. Aggregate tract data only. No download.

  Researcher (login): All public views. CSV download. API endpoint.
    Data dictionary and pipeline documentation page.
    Authentication: streamlit-authenticator (short-term) or Agnes OAuth.

  Clinician / Care Coordinator (Agnes login): All public views.
    Single-tract neighborhood context for a patient's address.
    Social determinant flags for care planning.
    Authentication: Agnes platform SSO (OAuth2 / OpenID Connect).

  All data shown is aggregate (census tract level); no individual data.

================================================================================
  4. SINGLE DASHBOARD VS. MULTIPLE DASHBOARDS
================================================================================

  Current situation:
    Baltimore_Dashboard_Updated.py: 91-indicator Streamlit app.
    Baltimore_MetricsWithMap.py: Capstone Flask app (3 indicators + chatbot).
    blsandfreddata.streamlit.app: BLS/FRED time series (partially broken).

  Recommendation: Consolidate into one Streamlit application.
    - One URL for users, citations, and Agnes integration.
    - Merge BLS/FRED as a "Regional Economic Trends" tab.
    - Port the Agno chatbot into the Streamlit app.
    - Retire Baltimore_MetricsWithMap.py (Flask) once Streamlit has parity.

================================================================================
  5. AGNES CANOPY INTEGRATION PLAN
================================================================================

Agnes is an open-source care coordination platform (Open Source Harbor).
Components: coordination dashboard, app registry, Orbit (platform-hosted model),
authentication, consent management, security.
Data standards: FHIR (clinical), OMOP (research / cross-site).

Canopy = population health and economic intelligence module of Agnes.
Connects as a Level 2 app via FHIR. Provides city/neighborhood context
to Agnes users looking at individual patient or research data.

Phased integration:

  Phase 0 (now): Standalone Streamlit app. No Agnes connection.

  Phase 1: Register in Agnes app registry. Canopy appears in coordination
    dashboard. Users click to open in new tab. No data flow yet.

  Phase 2: Agnes SSO. Canopy accepts login tokens from Agnes.
    Clinician role activates; Agnes passes patient census tract to Canopy.

  Phase 3: Orbit integration. Hosted model queries Canopy data as a tool.
    Example: coordinator asks Orbit about a patient's neighborhood; Orbit
    calls Canopy API and returns tract-level indicators.

  Phase 4: Community Voice. Resident-reported data visible in Canopy.

Technical prerequisites:
  - Stable deployed URL (done).
  - REST API endpoint: /api/tract/{geoid} returning JSON of all indicators.
  - Agnes app registry support (Agnes dev team, future).
